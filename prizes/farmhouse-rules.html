Farmhouse Rules

  1. Your submission may be on any subject involving the life of Richard Nixon, but it must include some discussion of his childhood at the Yorba Linda farmhouse, and it must be a personal essay. It may have footnotes. You may and are encouraged to utilize the writings of Richard Nixon. You may utilize secondary or research materials. The result, however, must be a piece of creative nonfiction and not a research paper.
  2. You may include your bio with your submission. Include at least a short statement, something about yourself, and how you came to write the essay.
  3. Your submission must include a self-addressed envelope with correct postage affixed.
  4. The prize in the amount of five hundred dollars will be awarded in the sole judgment of the editor. The editor reserves the right to award more than one prize, one prize, or no prize at all.
  5. Colonus Publishing, Inc., acquires all electronic rights.
  6. No simultaneous submissions.
  7. Your submission and any materials enclosed with your submission will not be returned. Please send only copies.
  8. The editor reserves the right to amend or supplement these rules to the Farmhouse.
